About Me

My name is Jacob Cravey and I was born and raised in Tucson Arizona. I graduated from Marana High School in 2010, and joined the army shortly thereafter. I served for four years as an Armament/Electrical/Avionic Systems Repairer for the AH-64 Apache Attack Helicopter, where I started getting a background in both analog and digital microelectronics.

After finishing my time in the Army, I bounced around from various jobs including construction, retail and even community association management. I finally decided it was time to do better, and I started an Associates in Information Technology with University of Phoenix. Part way through my degree, I found an opening at Apex Microtechnology, as an equipment maintenance tech. Having some background in electronics, I was able to get the job.

While working for Apex, I quickly proved myself and was placed into a process engineering role. While doing this I was exposed to some basic programming, ladder logic and even Visual Basic, and fell in love with programming. After finishing my Associates I decided to persue an education in programming and coding. I quickly found the U of A bootcamp, and now, here we are!
